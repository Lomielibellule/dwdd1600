Who Were the Signers

Famous Signers

Here are some of the most famous signers of the Declaration of Independence (1776):

John Hancock
President of the Second Continental Congress.
First (and largest) signature on the Declaration of Independence.
His bold signature made his name synonymous with “signature” in American slang.
Thomas Jefferson
Principal author of the Declaration of Independence.
Later became the 3rd President of the United States.
Advocated strongly for individual rights and liberty.
Benjamin Franklin
Inventor, diplomat, and writer.
Helped secure French support during the Revolutionary War.
One of the most internationally respected Founding Fathers.
John Adams
Strong advocate for independence.
Key leader in Congress pushing for the break from Britain.
Later became the 2nd President of the United States.
Samuel Adams
Leader of the Sons of Liberty.
Organizer of resistance to British rule in Massachusetts.
Cousin of John Adams.
