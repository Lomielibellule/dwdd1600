Digital Citizenship

What is Digital Citizenship?

Digital citizenship is the responsible, safe, and ethical use of technology and the internet. It includes respecting others online, protecting personal information, and following laws and rules when using digital tools.

What is digital law?

Digital law refers to the rules and regulations that govern the use of technology and the internet. This includes laws about copyright, privacy, cybercrime, online harassment, and data protection.

What is AUP?

AUP stands for Acceptable Use Policy. It is a set of rules that explain how technology, networks, or devices may be used appropriately, often in schools or workplaces.

What is plagarism?

Plagiarism is using someone else's words, ideas, or work without giving proper credit and presenting it as your own.

What are the rules for AI-generated content?

Rules for AI-generated content vary by institution, but generally include:

-Being transparent about using AI

-Not presenting AI-generated work as entirely your own if disclosure is required

-Following school or workplace policies

-Ensuring the content is original, accurate, and ethical
